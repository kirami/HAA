GRADING AND CONDITION CODES
All records are graded in the following manner. The record is assigned an overall condition grade, and then it is examined again for any specific defects. All records are graded by sight, with aural grading used in uncertain cases. We grade conservatively, but if you feel a record does not meet its assigned grade, let us know. Your satisfaction is guaranteed. Sorry, no adjustments on items graded “poor”.
These grading codes apply to the overall condition of the record:
e = excellent, nearly new, minor defects only, plays loud and clear.
v = very good, above average, a little wear and a few scuffs, plays well.
g = good, average for its age, audible scratches and wear, plays OK but noisy.
f = fair, below average, heavy wear or scratches, plays with difficulty.
p = poor, barely playable if at all, offered for visual interest only.
(+) = a little better (-) = a little worse
These grading codes apply to specific defects, regardless of overall condition:
The following defects are cosmetic only, and are not audible:
sol - sticker on label
ldm - label damaged
wol - writing on label
ulc - under label crack
nrl - needle run on label
shf - spindle hole flake
The following defects may cause an audible sound but should not affect tracking:
lam - lamination crack
gry - grey grooves
scr - scratch
ndr - needle run
hlc - hairline crack
grn - grainy surface
bbl - pressing bubble
wdm - water damage
crk - crack
hdm - heat damage
rst - rough start
scf - scuff
The following defects may cause mistracking, repeating or skipping grooves:
rch - rim chip
rcr - radial crack
rfl - rim flake
pit - pit or needle drop
wrp - warp
sco - scored grooves
The following defects apply to cylinder records only:
spl - split
pls - plaster missing
mld - mold
These suffix codes indicate the extent of the defect:
s(1) - side (1)
pot - potential
(1)" - (1) inch
ntg - not to grooves
(1)g - (1) groove
nap - not affecting play
The following cosmetic defects apply to albums, sleeves and covers:
soc - sticker on cover
scs - split cover seam
woc - writing on cover
adm - album damaged
toc - tape on cover
sub - substitute album
Double face records reissued from earlier single face issues will show the original single face numbers, when known, in parenthesis after the listing. Example: (sf 1234/1235).
Records reissued on a different label will show the original label and number, when known, in parenthesis after the listing. Example: (Victor 1234/1235).
We occasionally add a little information to a listing when there is something unique or special about a record. Please feel free to contact us if you need any further information about any item in the auction.
