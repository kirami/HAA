THIS IS A STRAIGHT BID AUCTION: NO BUYER’S PREMIUM, NO BID REDUCTION, NO TOPPING OR HIGH/LOW BIDS, NO TRADES, NO EXCEPTIONS. WHAT YOU BID IS WHAT YOU PAY.
BIDDING RULES: First of all, SAVE THIS LIST! Shortly after the closing date, all winning bidders will receive an invoice for the total amount of their winnings. We will also send each bidder a report of items bid on but not won, and the winning bid for each. You will need this list to refer to the lot numbers.
  1. If you’re bidding by mail, please use the bid sheet.
    Bids on odd scraps of paper can cause data entry errors. If you need more room than the bid sheet provides, please use a second sheet of paper.
  2. Minimum bids.
    A minimum bid is specified for each section of the auction and is effective for all items in that section unless otherwise noted. NMB means there is no minimum bid on a particular item. MB followed by a number means the minimum bid is higher or lower. (Example: MB7 means that the minimum bid is $7.00).
  3. Conditional bids.
    We can accept conditional bids, "either/or" bids, and you may set a limit on your winnings. Please keep the conditions simple and be sure to indicate them clearly on the bid sheet. Sorry, but we can not accept percentage, high/low, or "topping" bids.
  4. Modifying or withdrawing bids.
    You may modify or withdraw any of your bids anytime before the closing date of the auction. However, we do not disclose the status of any bid.
  5. Payments.
    We accept Visa, Mastercard and Paypal. Full payment for your winnings is required within 30 days of the date of the invoice. If we don't hear from you within that time, your records will be offered to other bidders or returned to our inventory and your name will be removed from our mailing list.
  6. Holding winnings.
    If you win only a few records, we can hold your winnings upon request and ship them with winnings from a future auction. We must receive full payment, less postage, plus any sales tax before records can be put on hold. Please note we will not hold records longer than 12 months.
SHIPPING: Shipping charges include sturdy mailing boxes, new packing material, and postage at the Media Mail rate (records and books) or Parcel Post rate (other items) in the USA and Air Mail overseas. All parcels are insured against loss or breakage. Alternate shipping methods quoted upon request only.
  1. USA:
    Minimum $6.00 per parcel, up to two pounds. Additional shipping charges will be based on increased postage costs as well as additional packing material needed for larger shipments.
  2. Canada and Overseas:
    Shipping cost varies with each country. Overseas parcels weighing less than 4 pounds and valued below $100 receive the best postage rate. All parcels are sent Air Mail.
In the unlikely event of your shipment being damaged, please contact us immediately. Do not return any items to us without our authorization and do not discard anything. All shipments are fully covered for loss or damage, and with your cooperation we will gladly make sure you receive full reimbursement.
CUSTOMERS OUTSIDE THE USA - you may be charged import duties based on the value of your shipment. We are sorry if this increases your cost, but we do not declare reduced values as we must comply with United States Customs regulations. Thank you for your cooperation.
GRADING AND CONDITION CODES All records are graded in the following manner. The record is assigned an overall condition grade, and then it is examined again for any specific defects. All records are graded by sight, with aural grading used in uncertain cases. We grade conservatively, but if you feel a record does not meet its assigned grade, let us know. Your satisfaction is guaranteed. Sorry, no adjustments on items graded “poor”.
These grading codes apply to the overall condition of the record:
e = excellent, nearly new, minor defects only, plays loud and clear.
v = very good, above average, a little wear and a few scuffs, plays well.
g = good, average for its age, audible scratches and wear, plays OK but noisy.
f = fair, below average, heavy wear or scratches, plays with difficulty.
p = poor, barely playable if at all, offered for visual interest only.
(+) = a little better (-) = a little worse
These grading codes apply to specific defects, regardless of overall condition:

The following defects may cause mistracking, repeating or skipping grooves:

The following defects apply to cylinder records only:

These suffix codes indicate the extent of the defect:
s(1) - side (1)
pot - potential
(1)" - (1) inch
ntg - not to grooves
(1)g - (1) groove
nap - not affecting play
The following cosmetic defects apply to albums, sleeves and covers:
soc - sticker on cover
scs - split cover seam
woc - writing on cover
adm - album damaged
toc - tape on cover
sub - substitute album
Double face records reissued from earlier single face issues will show the original single face numbers, when known, in parenthesis after the listing. Example: (sf 1234/1235).
Records reissued on a different label will show the original label and number, when known, in parenthesis after the listing. Example: (Victor 1234/1235).
